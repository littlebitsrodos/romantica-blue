# Sea Tree Design Brief

## North Star

Sea Tree is not trying to attract the most bookings. It is trying to attract the right 2-3 repeat guests: people with taste, respect, time, and the desire to return every year until the house feels like part of their life.

The site should feel like a personal invitation to live the rhythm of Aliki, not a generic vacation-rental funnel.

## Positioning

Sea Tree is a personal seaside home on the quiet side of Aliki, Paros, made for longer stays, slow days, quiet work, real rest, and returning guests.

Core promise:

> A place where, after a few days, your body learns the rhythm of the island.

Support the promise with concrete truths:

- You hear the waves.
- You see the traditional boats across Aliki bay.
- You are on the quiet side of Aliki beach.
- The village, tavernas, sandy swimming beach, and daily essentials are a short walk away.
- The house is made for more than summer, with year-round comfort including a fireplace.
- The home has tactile character, especially its kourasani surfaces.

## Audience

Primary audience:

- Couples, small families, or individuals aged roughly 35-65.
- High-quality European or North American guests.
- People who value quiet, taste, authenticity, and a real-home feeling.
- Guests considering 2-5 week stays and potential annual returns.
- Remote workers are welcome, but the site should not feel like a digital-nomad product.

Avoid optimizing for:

- Party-oriented stays.
- Bargain hunters.
- Fast 2-3 night impulse trips.
- People asking only for monthly discounts.
- Generic luxury-hotel expectations.

## Tone

Personal, story-led, precise, and calm.

The voice should feel like someone who loves the house saying: if this is the rhythm you are looking for, you may belong here.

Avoid:

- Over-polished resort language.
- "Luxury villa" cliches.
- Discount-led long-stay language.
- Overplaying the Romantica disco history as a gimmick.

## Message Hierarchy

Lead with the present-day experience:

1. Quiet waterfront rhythm.
2. Village life beside the sea.
3. A real home for longer stays.
4. Year-round comfort.
5. The Romantica story as a second layer of soul and memory.

The Romantica history matters, but it should not be the first impression. The first impression is the calm seaside home.

## Location Truth

Be clear and honest:

- The house is directly by the water.
- The shore directly in front is rocky and not for swimming.
- This keeps the immediate frontage especially quiet.
- The sandy swimming beach is about a two-minute walk away.
- Tavernas, supermarket, and village essentials are nearby at village scale.

Recommended copy:

> Directly by the water, with the sandy swimming beach two minutes away.

Detailed copy:

> The shore directly in front of the house is rocky and not used for swimming, which keeps this side of Aliki beach especially quiet. The sandy swimming area, tavernas, supermarket, and village life are a short two-minute walk away.

## Pricing Truth

Long stay does not mean discounted stay.

Do not write:

- monthly discount
- extended-stay deal
- long-stay offer
- special monthly pricing

Recommended FAQ copy:

> Rates are based on the season and requested dates rather than stay length. Sea Tree is especially well suited to longer stays because of its comfort, quiet location, work setup, and village rhythm.

## Conversion Strategy

Primary conversion:

> Tell us about your longer stay.

Use a structured long-stay inquiry form. It should ask:

- Name
- Email
- Desired dates
- Length of stay
- Guest count
- Whether they plan to work remotely
- What kind of stay they are imagining

Secondary conversion:

> Prefer to speak directly? Message Antonis on WhatsApp.

Platform fallback:

> Prefer a platform booking? Sea Tree is also listed on Airbnb and Booking.com.

Airbnb and Booking.com remain trust and booking channels, but the site should primarily guide good-fit long-stay guests toward direct contact.

## Section Architecture

Recommended order:

1. Hero
2. The rhythm of staying here
3. The home
4. Longer stays
5. Life in Aliki
6. The Romantica story
7. Practical comforts
8. Availability
9. A note from Antonis
10. Long-stay inquiry

## Hero Direction

Visual:

- Terrace, water, boats, quiet side of Aliki.
- Prefer a still image over video for performance.
- Use the strongest image that communicates "quiet waterfront rhythm" before interior detail.

Copy direction:

> Live slowly by the water in Aliki, Paros.

Alternative:

> A personal seaside home on the quiet side of Aliki.

Supporting copy:

> Wake to the sound of the waves, watch the traditional boats across the bay, and walk two minutes to the sandy beach, tavernas, and village essentials.

Primary CTA:

> Tell us about your longer stay

Secondary CTA:

> Explore the home

## Rhythm Section

This should come immediately after the hero. It sells the product before the amenities.

Use four scenes:

- Morning by the water: waves, light, boats.
- Village days: bakery, supermarket, tavernas, sandy beach.
- Quiet work, real rest: Starlink, desk, calm corners.
- Evenings that slow down: terrace, dinner, bay light, no rush.

Reference copy:

> At Sea Tree, Aliki becomes part of your day: a walk by the bay, boats in the evening light, the village close enough for everything you need, and quiet enough to let time stretch.

## Life in Aliki

Aliki should be more than "location." It is part of the promise.

Frame it as village-scale daily life:

> Aliki gives you the essentials of daily life at village pace: a bakery, supermarket, pharmacy, waterfront tavernas, and beaches you can walk to.

Do not imply full urban convenience.

## Year-Round Home

Sea Tree is not only a summer rental.

Recommended copy:

> Made for more than summer, Sea Tree is a year-round seaside home, with a fireplace, quiet work corners, and the comfort to settle in for longer.

Use this in the longer-stays or practical-comforts section, not as the main hero message.

## Host Presence

Include a personal host section, likely near contact.

Title:

> A note from Antonis

Direction:

The note should explain that Sea Tree is not just a place to rent, but a home the owners want to share with people who understand its rhythm.

It should invite guests to describe the kind of stay they imagine.

## Visual Direction

Prioritize images that show rhythm and texture, not only real-estate coverage.

Image types needed:

- Terrace and water.
- Boats across Aliki bay.
- Quiet side of the beach.
- The walk from the house to the sandy beach.
- Kourasani surfaces and tactile details.
- Kitchen, table, terrace, and everyday corners.
- Workspace with light or sea view.
- Fireplace and off-season warmth.
- Village-scale life around Aliki.

The owner rhythm video is useful as creative reference: calm shallow water, small boats, sandy shoreline, soft evening light, and village buildings nearby without a busy resort feeling.

## Design Principles

- Editorial first, practical when it matters.
- Let the first screens create feeling; let later sections answer questions.
- Use tactile, human-scale details.
- Be precise about tradeoffs instead of hiding them.
- Make the site select for fit, not maximize every possible lead.
- Keep the design calm, spacious, and confident.
- Avoid visual noise, generic luxury tropes, and over-touristic Paros imagery.
